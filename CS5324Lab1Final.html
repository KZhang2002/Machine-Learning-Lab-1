1. Business Understanding

With the rise of Criminal Justice reform and the Black Lives Matter movement, the US justice system has become a point of interest for many Americans. America also has large amount of prisons. Prison Policy Initiative found that Together, these systems hold almost 2 million people in 1,566 state prisons, 98 federal prisons, 3,116 local jails, 1,323 juvenile correctional facilities, 181 immigration detention facilities, and 80 Indian country jails, as well as in military prisons, civil commitment centers, state psychiatric hospitals, and prisons in the U.S. territories (Sawyer & Wagner, 2023). Thus, if someone does not sit behind bars themselves, it is still highly likely that the United States prison system has had an impact on their lives.

This data set was populated from open source search methodologies of authoritative sources, which is a way of using established and credible techniques to search for public yet reliable information. It was originally populated to describe the extent of where the incarcerated population is located and to describe many physical and social characteristics of detention facilities in the United States ("Prison Boundaries", 2020). To clarify and hone in on important information that may be categorized as identity or location, several attributes have been dropped from the following analysis. The facility's id, state, type, population, security level, and capacity have all been kept.

The predictive task is illustrating which states need more or less prisons based upon the comparison of the population and capacity attributes. The analysis may be deemed useful to third parties when the locations that not only hold the most prisons but the most inmates are analyzed as this may allow citizens to better understand the overcrowding processes of prisons and may direct movement leaders and prison owners to specific areas of the country.

Since this data set has large amount of columns (32), columns deemed extraneous were dropped. Entries deemed outside the scope of this paper have also been dropped. These include prisons that are located in US territories but not in the US itself.

The security level of the prisons was also changed from qualitative to quantitative, with values "Juvenile", "Minimum", "Medium", and "Maximum" being replaced with values 1 through 4, respectively.

As described in the metadata of the dataset, missing values were not given NaN values. Instead missing string values were represented by a "Not Available" string while empty numerical values were represented by the value of -999. These values have been replaced with NaN values to aid with detecting missing data.

3.1.4 Amount of Prisons Versus Population per State

In[9]:
# Get the counts of each state
state_counts = df_pb.groupby('STATE').size().reset_index(name='Counts')

# Merge state_counts and df_pop based on the 'STATE' column, pc = popcount
df_pc = pd.merge(state_counts, df_pop, on='STATE', how='inner')

# Calculate z-scores for X and Y
z_scores_x = (df_pc['Population'] - df_pc['Population'].mean()) / df_pc['Population'].std()
z_scores_y = (df_pc['Counts'] - df_pc['Counts'].mean()) / df_pc['Counts'].std()

# Identify outliers based on the z-score threshold
z_threshold = 1.5
outliers = df_pc[(np.abs(z_scores_x) > z_threshold) | (np.abs(z_scores_y) > z_threshold)]

# Plot scatterplot
plt.figure(figsize=(18, 6))
plt.scatter(y = df_pc.Counts, x = df_pc.Population, label='Standard Data Point')
plt.scatter(outliers['Population'], outliers['Counts'], color='red', label='Outliers')
plt.legend()

# Create regression line using polynomial fit
coefficients = np.polyfit(df_pc.Population, df_pc.Counts, 1)
slope, intercept = coefficients
regression_line = [slope * xi + intercept for xi in df_pc.Population]

# Plot the regression line
plt.plot(df_pc.Population, regression_line, color='red', label='Line of Best Fit')

# Label each point with the corresponding state name
for i, state in enumerate(df_pc.STATE): 
    plt.text(df_pc.Population[i], df_pc.Counts[i], state, fontsize=12, ha='right', va='bottom')

# Add labels to plot
plt.ylabel("Number of prisons")
plt.xlabel("State population")
plt.title("Amount of Prisons Versus Population per State")

plt.show();

Figure 4: It is makes sense that California, Texas, Florida, and New York would be outliers since they have the largest populations. However, Georgia is unique in that it's status as an outlier is due to its large amount of prisons rather than its population. This confirms our suspicions from the last graph. It can be interpereted that states above the regression line have too many prisons while states under the regression line need more prisons in order to avoid overcrowding. However, the regression line may have been skewed by our outliers.
